Our Curriculum

Competency-Based Curriculum

Lynpark Learning Centre fully implements Kenya's Competency-Based Curriculum (CBC), designed to nurture every learner's talents and abilities through competency-based assessment rather than examination-focused learning.

The CBC framework focuses on developing seven core competencies: Communication and Collaboration, Critical Thinking and Problem Solving, Creativity and Imagination, Citizenship, Digital Literacy, Learning to Learn, and Self-Efficacy.

Critical Thinking

Problem-solving and analytical skills

Digital Literacy

Technology integration in learning

Creativity

Arts, innovation, and imagination

Collaboration

Teamwork and communication

Citizenship

Values, patriotism, and social responsibility

Self-Efficacy

Confidence and personal growth

PP1 & PP2

Pre-Primary Program

Our Pre-Primary program provides a warm, stimulating environment where children aged 4-6 begin their learning journey. Through play-based activities, children develop foundational literacy, numeracy, and social skills that prepare them for formal schooling.

  • Language activities & storytelling
  • Number work & mathematical concepts
  • Creative arts, music & movement
  • Environmental awareness
  • Psychomotor & social skills
LanguageMathematicsCreative ArtsMusicEnvironmental
Grade 1 — 3

Lower Primary Program

The Lower Primary level builds strong foundations in literacy and numeracy while introducing learners to the broader world through environmental activities and digital literacy. Our teachers use hands-on, activity-based approaches to make learning engaging and meaningful.

  • Literacy — English & Kiswahili
  • Numeracy & mathematics
  • Environmental activities
  • Hygiene & nutrition
  • Religious education (CRE/IRE)
  • Digital literacy basics
EnglishKiswahiliMathematicsScienceCREDigital Literacy
Grade 4 — 6

Upper Primary Program

Upper Primary learners delve deeper into subject areas with increased academic rigor. Critical thinking, research skills, and collaborative projects become central to the learning process. Students are assessed through formative assessments that measure competency growth.

  • English & Kiswahili literature
  • Mathematics & pre-algebra
  • Science & technology
  • Social studies & citizenship
  • Creative arts & sports
  • Agriculture & life skills
EnglishKiswahiliMathematicsScienceSocial StudiesAgricultureCreative Arts
Grade 7 — 8

Junior Secondary Program

The Junior Secondary level prepares learners for specialised pathways in Senior Secondary. Students explore career interests through practical subjects and are guided in choosing pathways — Arts & Sports, Social Sciences, or STEM — that align with their strengths and aspirations.

  • Core subjects (English, Kiswahili, Maths, Sciences)
  • Pre-technical studies & business
  • STEM pathway exploration
  • Performing arts & visual arts
  • Community service learning
  • Career guidance & mentorship
STEMBusinessArtsLanguagesSocial SciencesSports
Beyond The Classroom

Co-Curricular Activities

We believe in holistic development. Our vibrant co-curricular program helps students discover their passions and build life skills.

Sports

Football, athletics, netball, volleyball, and swimming. We compete at sub-county and county levels.

Music & Drama

Choir, traditional dance, drama club, and musical instruments. Regular performances at school events.

Art & Craft

Drawing, painting, sculpture, and crafts. Students showcase their work in termly exhibitions.

Debate & Public Speaking

Building confidence, critical thinking, and communication through competitive debates and speeches.

Scouts & Guides

Character building, outdoor skills, community service, and leadership development.

Environmental Club

Tree planting, recycling, clean-up drives, and conservation awareness. Nurturing eco-conscious citizens.
